# Geographical Expansion Plan

**Project:** WanderAI — AI Travel Itinerary Planner
**Author:** Aryan Agarwal
**Status:** Hackathon submission — forward-looking growth plan

## How I plan to expand reach and onboard users, with horizontal expansion geographically

WanderAI already supports **English, Hindi, and Telugu** — so expansion is
designed to follow the languages the product can already serve, rather than
trying to be everywhere at once.

**Phase 1 — Telugu-speaking states (Telangana & Andhra Pradesh):**
Launch first where the Telugu interface gives a real edge over English-only
competitors (MakeMyTrip, generic travel blogs). Target college campuses,
local travel Facebook/Instagram groups, and budget-traveller WhatsApp
communities in Hyderabad, Vijayawada, and Visakhapatnam.

**Phase 2 — Hindi-speaking belt (Delhi-NCR, UP, MP, Rajasthan, Bihar):**
Once Phase 1 generates enough usage and reviews to validate the product, push
the Hindi interface into the much larger Hindi-speaking user base, using the
same low-cost channels (student groups, Reddit, travel forums).

**Phase 3 — Pan-India, additional languages:**
Based on which destinations and languages users keep asking about in the
review section, add the next 1–2 languages (likely Tamil or Marathi/Bengali,
whichever shows the most demand signal) and expand outreach to those regions.

**Phase 4 — Indian diaspora / NRIs:**
The English version already works for NRIs planning trips back to India or
elsewhere — outreach here is through NRI travel subreddits and Facebook
groups, where the ₹-first budgeting is still useful for people earning in
foreign currency but spending in India.

**Phase 5 — Adjacent South Asian markets (longer-term):**
The core insight behind WanderAI — converting confusing local/USD pricing
into a traveller's home currency — applies just as well to Bangladesh (BDT),
Sri Lanka (LKR), or Nepal (NPR). This is the natural next horizontal step
once the India rollout is stable, by swapping the currency-conversion logic
in the prompt rather than rebuilding the product.
